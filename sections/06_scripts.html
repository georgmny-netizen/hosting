Limitation of Liability

The total aggregate liability of the Seller arising out of or in connection with any contract, delivery, or batch of goods shall in no event exceed the invoice value of the specific batch of goods to which the claim relates.

Under no circumstances shall the Seller be liable for indirect, incidental, special, punitive or consequential damages, including loss of production, loss of profit, loss of contracts, facility downtime, business interruption or reputational damage.

The Buyer’s sole and exclusive remedy shall be limited to replacement of the affected goods or refund of the invoice value of the affected batch, subject to the RMA & Quality Dispute Policy.

The Seller shall not be liable for delay or failure caused by events beyond reasonable control, including APAC port congestion, customs clearance delay, export/import control action, carrier disruption, force majeure, geopolitical restriction or regulatory intervention.

Procedure

RMA & Quality Dispute

  • Buyer must submit a written claim within 10 calendar days from delivery.
  • Claim must include batch number, CoA reference, receiving condition, photographs and buyer test results with method declaration.
  • Material must be quarantined, unused and preserved in original packaging where possible.
  • Seller completes CoA, traceability and internal QC log review within 48 hours.
  • Dispute is escalated to independent laboratory arbitration if results diverge.
Independent laboratory

Binding Lab Arbitration

SGS, Eurofins or another mutually accepted ISO/IEC 17025 accredited laboratory acts as neutral analytical authority. Sample split: Buyer retain sample / Seller retain sample / Lab reference sample. Method alignment: GDMS or ICP-MS as specified in the CoA.

If Seller fault is confirmed: replacement or refund. If buyer handling, contamination or unauthorized processing is confirmed: claim rejected. If inconclusive: commercial settlement by written agreement.

Legal trap

How a large buyer can bankrupt the supplier

If the contract is silent on consequential damages, the buyer may claim line stoppage, lost wafer output, customer penalties, expedited replacement, reputational damage and internal investigation costs. A single delayed or downgraded lot can become a claim far above invoice value.

  • Danger phrase: ?�Supplier shall be liable for all losses arising from delay or nonconformity.
  • Danger gap: no inspection window, no exclusive remedy, no cap, no exclusion of indirect damages.
  • Danger evidence: website claims ?�guaranteed supply” or ?�zero risk” without contract limits.

Except for fraud, willful misconduct, proven sanctions violation caused solely by Supplier, or breach of confidentiality, Supplier’s aggregate liability arising out of or relating to any purchase order, shipment, delay, nonconforming material, documentation error or related service shall be limited to the amount paid by Buyer for the affected lot.     Joint Lab Testing / Round Robin Protocol

    Procedure
    1. Batch sample split into Seller / Buyer / Reference Lab units.
    2. Each unit receives identical Trace ID and tamper seal record.
    3. Seller and buyer test using aligned GDMS / ICP-MS method.
    4. Deviation above tolerance triggers third-party reference lab.
    5. Method correlation report is archived in VDR.
    Dispute prevention rule

    Result deviation > ±15% at ppb level requires method reconciliation, sample handling review and calibration evidence comparison before any quality rejection is accepted.

    Reference laboratory result becomes binding if both parties signed the Round Robin protocol.
